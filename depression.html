Understanding Depression

About...

There are multiple types of depression. Factors that can cause depression include personal or family history of depression, trauma, stress, and certain medications. Common syptoms and signs of depression include persistent low mood, sense of despair, and sadness. Physical symptoms can include aches, cramps, headaches, difficulty sleeping and weight changes. See a medical profession for further help and treatment methods.

Types

Bipolar Depression: someone experiences episodes of extreme low moods and extremely high moods.

Persistent Depressive Disorder or Dysthymia: consistent depressed mood for at least two years, and displays at least two of the following symptoms: poor appetite or overeating; insomnia or excessive sleep; low energy or fatigue; low self-esteem; poor concentration or indecisiveness; and hopelessness.

Major Depression: similar symptoms to dysthymia including depressed mood, low self-esteem, and hopelessness.Major depression may also include two symptoms not found in dysthymia: anhedonia or the inability to feel pleasure and lethargy. An episode of major depression requires at least five symptoms and needs tp last only two weeks rather than two years.
